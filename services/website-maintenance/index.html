What Maintenance Can Include

A website is a working business asset. Maintenance keeps it accurate, stable, secure, and easier to improve after launch.

Content updates

Update services, team details, images, pricing notes, calls to action, contact details, and location information.

Bug fixes

Fix broken layouts, form issues, mobile problems, missing assets, link errors, and small problems before they grow.

Security checks

Review access, dependencies, server behaviour, suspicious activity, and basic hardening needs where relevant.

SEO monitoring

Check Search Console, sitemap visibility, indexing changes, metadata issues, and pages that need improvement.

Performance checks

Watch page weight, image loading, caching, mobile usability, and avoidable layout or loading issues.

Reporting

Keep a simple record of completed work, issues found, recommendations, and next actions for the site.

Why Maintenance Matters

Most websites slowly drift out of date. Services change, links break, staff details move, forms stop being tested, and Search Console starts showing warnings nobody is watching.

Maintenance gives the site a rhythm. Small fixes, content improvements, and monitoring happen before they become urgent. That is especially useful for businesses that rely on their website for enquiries.

  • StabilityRegular checks help keep forms, pages, links, scripts, and key customer journeys working.
  • AccuracyService information, contact details, offers, and calls to action can stay aligned with the business.
  • VisibilitySEO and indexing checks help catch technical problems that can quietly reduce search performance.
  • MomentumMaintenance creates space for small improvements instead of waiting for a full redesign.

Website Maintenance FAQs

How often should a website be maintained?

Most business websites should be checked at least monthly. Sites with frequent content changes, forms, SEO work, or application features may need more regular support.

Can maintenance include SEO?

Yes. Maintenance can include Search Console checks, sitemap review, metadata improvements, internal link updates, performance checks, and recommendations for pages that need stronger content.

Can you maintain a site you did not build?

Usually yes. We first review the site structure, access, hosting, codebase, and risk level so we understand what can be safely supported.

Does maintenance replace a redesign?

No. Maintenance keeps a good site healthy and improving. If the structure, design, or content is fundamentally weak, a redesign may still be the better long-term fix.
